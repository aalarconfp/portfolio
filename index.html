Cash & Liquidity
13-Week Cash Flow Forecast
Problem

Most companies only find out they're low on cash once the month is already over — because they look at what already happened, not what's coming.

↓

Early warning before cash gets tight, and a simple weekly view you can review at a glance.

03 / How I Think About Finance Work
Principle I
Get the numbers right before making them look good
A fast report built on bad numbers is worse than no report — it gives you false confidence when you're making a decision. Before building anything, I always ask: what does it take for this number to be trustworthy, every single month? That answer shapes how the whole system is built, not just how it looks.
Principle II
Fix the process first, then build the dashboard
A dashboard should be the last step of a well-run process, not a shortcut around one. Systems built backwards — starting with a pretty chart and figuring out the process later — tend to break once real work piles up. Before writing a single formula, I map out where the data comes from, who owns it, how it's categorized, and who checks it.
Principle III
Simple systems are stronger than complicated ones
Financial controls fail when they're too complicated for the people running them to use consistently. The best systems are structured enough to grow with the business, simple enough that anyone can double-check them, and clear enough that if something looks off, you'll spot it right away — no guesswork needed.
